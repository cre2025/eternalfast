# Fastener Surface Treatment and Salt Spray Basics

Surface treatment is a key factor in corrosion resistance and long-term reliability, especially for export and infrastructure projects.

## Common Surface Treatment Options

- **Zinc plating**: Cost-effective and widely used for general environments
- **Hot dip galvanizing (HDG)**: Thicker coating for outdoor and heavy corrosion exposure
- **Dacromet**: Common in high-corrosion applications
- **Ruspert**: Strong corrosion performance for demanding projects
- **Zinc-nickel**: Stable anti-corrosion behavior, often used in automotive and premium applications

## How to Read Salt Spray Results

Salt spray testing is commonly used to compare coating systems (often NSS testing).  
Higher test hours generally indicate better corrosion resistance, but real field life also depends on design, assembly quality, and service conditions.

## Recommended Selection Steps

1. Define service conditions (humidity, salinity, temperature, chemicals)
2. Define target service life
3. Confirm customer, industry, and project standards
4. Select coating strategy by performance and cost balance
5. Validate with sample tests (salt spray + assembly verification)

## Common Mistakes

- Looking only at salt spray hours without installation factors
- Ignoring friction effects of coating on tightening behavior
- Applying one coating strategy to all environments
