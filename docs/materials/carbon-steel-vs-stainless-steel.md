# Carbon Steel vs Stainless Steel Fasteners

Material choice directly affects joint strength, corrosion resistance, service life, and total lifecycle cost.

## Typical Use Cases

- New projects selecting bolt, nut, and washer materials
- Export sourcing that balances cost and durability
- Equipment operating in humidity, salt spray, heat, or vibration

## Quick Comparison

| Aspect | Carbon Steel Fasteners | Stainless Steel Fasteners |
|---|---|---|
| Cost | Usually lower | Usually higher |
| Strength range | Can cover 4.8 / 8.8 / 10.9 / 12.9 | Commonly A2-70 / A4-80 |
| Corrosion resistance | Depends on coating system | Stronger intrinsic corrosion resistance |
| Typical applications | General machinery and structural joints | Offshore, chemical, outdoor humid environments |

## Recommended Selection Logic

1. **Start with environment**: For long-term humidity, salt, or chemical exposure, prioritize corrosion-focused solutions.
2. **Then evaluate load**: For high preload and fatigue service, verify strength grade and torque window.
3. **Use lifecycle cost**: Include maintenance, replacement, and downtime impact, not only unit price.

## Common Mistakes

- Assuming stainless steel is always stronger than carbon steel
- Selecting only by price without corrosion/fatigue assessment
- Ignoring system matching across bolts, nuts, and washers

## Coating Strategy for Carbon Steel

Carbon steel solutions often rely on zinc plating, hot dip galvanizing, Dacromet, Ruspert, or Zn-Ni systems.  
Final choice should match target service life, corrosion class, and project standards.
