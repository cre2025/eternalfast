# Fastener Selection by Industry

This guide provides practical selection priorities for four high-demand sectors: solar, automotive, steel structure, and offshore.

## 1) Solar (PV Mounting and Balance of System)

### Typical Requirements

- Outdoor and long-term weather exposure
- Wind load and thermal cycling
- Reliable anti-loosening performance

### Selection Priorities

- Corrosion-resistant materials or coatings (stainless steel, Zn-Ni, HDG, project dependent)
- Stable preload strategy with compatible washers and locking methods
- Verified performance under dynamic load and environmental testing

### Common Risks

- Underestimating galvanic corrosion in mixed-metal assemblies
- Ignoring preload loss after thermal cycles

## 2) Automotive

### Typical Requirements

- High vibration and fatigue conditions
- Tight consistency windows for torque and friction
- Scalable quality control for mass production

### Selection Priorities

- Strength class matched to joint criticality (often 8.8/10.9/12.9 ranges)
- Coating systems with controlled friction behavior
- Full traceability, PPAP-style documentation, and stable process capability

### Common Risks

- Focusing only on static strength without fatigue validation
- Mixing components from different friction systems

## 3) Steel Structure

### Typical Requirements

- High-load structural joints
- Strict preload and installation procedures
- Long service life in variable environments

### Selection Priorities

- Structural-grade bolting systems aligned with project standards
- Controlled tightening method and installation quality records
- Corrosion strategy based on service class (indoor/outdoor/coastal)

### Common Risks

- Using general-purpose fasteners in structural joints
- Missing installation training and torque-tension verification

## 4) Offshore and Marine

### Typical Requirements

- Severe chloride exposure and humidity
- High reliability under cyclic loading
- Long maintenance intervals and high failure cost

### Selection Priorities

- Corrosion-first design (material + coating + sealing strategy)
- Grade selection validated against fatigue and service conditions
- Enhanced traceability and inspection data package

### Common Risks

- Assuming one coating can solve all marine scenarios
- Skipping compatibility checks for adjacent materials

## Cross-Industry Quick Checklist

1. Define joint function and failure consequence
2. Confirm standards, dimensions, and property class
3. Select material and corrosion system by environment
4. Verify preload control strategy and assembly method
5. Lock quality plan, traceability, and acceptance criteria
