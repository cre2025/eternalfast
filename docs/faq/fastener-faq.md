# Fastener FAQ (Living Document)

## Q1: Can DIN and ISO parts be substituted directly?

Not always. Some references are equivalent, but dimensions, tolerances, mechanical properties, and marking rules can still differ.

## Q2: What do 8.8, 10.9, and 12.9 mean?

These are common property classes for carbon/alloy steel fasteners, linked to tensile and yield-related performance. Higher class is not automatically better for every application.

## Q3: Why can same-size fasteners have very different prices?

Price differences often come from material, grade, coating system, inspection scope, traceability requirements, and packaging/logistics standards.

## Q4: What should be selected for coastal or humid conditions?

Prioritize corrosion-focused solutions such as suitable stainless grades or higher-performance coating systems, then validate with testing and assembly checks.

## Q5: How long does custom fastener development usually take?

It depends on drawing complexity, tooling route, inspection requirements, and order scale. Prototype validation is strongly recommended before mass production.

## Q6: How can loosening risk be reduced?

Use a system approach: preload control, locking features, thread-locking solutions, process consistency, and maintenance strategy.

## Q7: Can you provide material certificates and batch traceability?

Yes. Final documentation scope depends on project requirements and customer specifications.
